Main Combat:
Enemies attack automatically, with obvious wind up. Player swings their weapon in the direction of the enemy's attack to deflect it.
Each deflect wears down the enemy's guard. When the enemy's guard is down they become stunned for a while and more vulnerable to attacks.
Once stun is over the enemy's guard goes back up.
Attacks on an enemy with guard up do damage, but an almost insignificant amount.

Blocking:
Players have block. Players can block by letting an enemy hit their shield, instead of hitting the enemy's weapon back with their own.
Players have a block stat, which determines how much damage they can block in any one fight.
Blocking is essentially an easy to use, but limited panic option for fights.

Attacking:
Players attack by slashing the enemy with their sword in any direction.
Attacking in specific directions in a specific order deals increased damage. These are called combos.

Magic:
Not necessary, but possible feature later in development.
Players press a button to activate an equipped spell. Time slows and the player has to do a hand motion pertaining to the spell.
Damage spells work regardless of the enemy's guard. Healing spells allow the player to recover mid-battle. 

The Map:
The player must make their way through a fortress until they slay the final boss at the end.

Progression: 
The player always gets some money after defeating enemies, and sometimes gets a choice of gear.
Money can be used to buy gear or heal between fights.

Bloodlines:
Each death moves the player onto the next member of their bloodline. Nothing is carried over, and the player must navigate the map again.
Enemies are respawned and randomized somewhat.

Stats:
Attack: Increases damage you do with your weapon
Defense: Reduces incoming damage
Block: Determines how much you can block
Magic: Increases magic effectiveness (damage dealt or amount healed)
